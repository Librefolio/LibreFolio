# 📊 Period P&L (Profit and Loss)

*[⬅️ Back to Performance Metrics Overview](index.md)*

## 💡 What is Period P&L?

The **Period P&L** (Profit and Loss) represents the absolute monetary gains or losses generated by your portfolio within the selected time window, adjusted for external cash flows.

It answers the direct question: _"How much money did I actually earn or lose during this period?"_

Unlike percentage-based metrics (such as [Simple ROI](roi.md) or [TWRR](twrr.md)), Period P&L is expressed as an absolute fiat amount (e.g., EUR, USD). Crucially, it is **cash-flow adjusted**, meaning it isolates actual investment performance from your deposits and withdrawals.

---

## 🧮 Formula

LibreFolio calculates the Period P&L using the following equation:

$$
\text{Period P}\&\text{L} = \text{NAV}_{\text{end}} - \text{NAV}_{\text{start}} - \text{Net External Flows}
$$

Where:

- **$\text{NAV}_{\text{start}}$**: The [Net Asset Value (Net Worth)](nav.md) at the beginning of the selected time window.
- **$\text{NAV}_{\text{end}}$**: The Net Asset Value at the end of the selected time window.
- **$\text{Net External Flows}$**: The net capital injected or removed by the investor during the period, defined as:

$$
\text{Net External Flows} = \text{Deposits} - \text{Withdrawals}
$$

Only flows that enter or exit the selected portfolio scope count as external. Internal transfers between brokers or accounts within the scope do not affect this calculation.

---

## 📝 Practical Example

Suppose your portfolio has the following metrics for a given year:

- **NAV at start**: €27,000
- **Total Deposits**: €1,000
- **Total Withdrawals**: €0
- **NAV at end**: €33,000

First, we calculate the Net External Flows:

$$
\text{Net External Flows} = 1,000 - 0 = \text{€}1,000
$$

Then, we calculate the Period P&L:

$$
\text{Period P}\&\text{L} = 33,000 - 27,000 - 1,000 = \text{€}5,000
$$

### 🔍 Explanation of the Result

Although the total valuation of your portfolio increased by **€6,000** (from €27,000 to €33,000), **€1,000** of that increase was your own money added. Therefore, your investments generated a real net gain of **€5,000**. 

If the formula did not adjust for external flows, it would incorrectly show a profit of €6,000, misleading you into thinking your assets performed better than they actually did.

---

## ⚖️ Key Differences

- **vs. ROI / TWRR / MWRR**: These are percentage-based metrics showing the rate of return. Period P&L shows the absolute monetary amount of profit/loss.
- **vs. Unrealized Gain/Loss**: Unrealized Gain/Loss is a snapshot of current open positions compared to their original purchase cost. Period P&L measures the performance of both open and closed positions (realized gains, dividends, interest) specifically within the bounds of the chosen time window.
